## Introduction

Welcome to SALIS AUTO! As a technician, your primary workspace is the Technician Portal — a dedicated interface designed for your daily work. This guide covers everything you need to know to work efficiently on the platform.

**Login:** Use your assigned email and password  
**Start Page:** After login, you are directed to `/technician-portal`

**Test Credentials:** tech@salisauto.com / tech123

## Chapter 1: Accessing the Technician Portal

### 1.1 Login Process
1. Open the SALIS AUTO URL in your browser
2. Enter your email address and password
3. If 2FA is enabled, enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app
4. You are redirected to the Technician Portal dashboard

### 1.2 Technician Portal Dashboard (`/technician-portal`)
Your home screen shows:
- **My Jobs Today** — jobs assigned to you for today
- **Pending Tasks** — incomplete tasks requiring action
- **My Performance** — your current efficiency rating
- **Announcements** — workshop updates from management

### 1.3 Mobile Access
For maximum convenience, use the Technician Mobile App at `/technician-app`. It's optimized for phone use with large touch targets and simplified navigation.

## Chapter 2: Working with Job Cards

### 2.1 Viewing Assigned Jobs (`/technician-portal/my-jobs`)
1. Click **My Jobs** in the sidebar
2. View the list of jobs assigned to you
3. Jobs are sorted by priority (Urgent → High → Normal → Low)
4. Click any job to view its details

### 2.2 Job Card Details
Each job card shows:
- **Customer name** and **vehicle information**
- **Service description** and requested repairs
- **Task list** with individual steps
- **Parts required** for the job
- **Estimated time** for completion
- **Special notes** from the service advisor

### 2.3 Updating Job Status
As you work through a job:
1. Click **Start Job** when you begin work
2. Update task completion: check off each task as you complete it
3. Record **actual time spent** vs. estimated
4. When all tasks are done, click **Complete Job**
5. Service advisor or QC inspector will then review before delivery

### 2.4 Logging Parts Used
1. Open the job card → **Parts** tab
2. Scan the barcode or search for the part
3. Enter quantity used
4. Click **Add Part** — inventory is automatically deducted

### 2.5 Adding Job Notes
1. Open job card → **Notes** tab
2. Type any technical observations
3. Click **Save Note**
4. Notes are visible to service advisors and managers

## Chapter 3: Time Clock (`/technician-portal/time-clock`)

### 3.1 Daily Clock In
1. Go to **Time Clock** in your portal
2. Click **Clock In** at the start of your shift
3. System records your start time
4. Always clock in before starting any work

### 3.2 Clock Out
1. Go to **Time Clock**
2. Click **Clock Out** when your shift ends
3. System records your hours worked
4. Review your daily hours summary

### 3.3 Break Recording
- Click **Start Break** when taking a break
- Click **End Break** when you return
- Breaks are tracked separately from work time

### 3.4 Viewing Your Timesheet
Go to **My Profile → Attendance** to view:
- Daily clock in/out records
- Weekly hours summary
- Overtime tracking
- Discrepancies to report to HR

## Chapter 4: Parts Lookup (`/technician-portal/parts`)

### 4.1 Searching for Parts
1. Go to **Parts** in your portal
2. Type part name, SKU, or compatible vehicle make/model
3. View availability at your branch
4. If not in stock, check network availability

### 4.2 Requesting Parts
If a part is not in stock:
1. Click **Request Part**
2. Specify quantity needed and job card
3. Purchase agent or parts manager receives the request
4. You are notified when parts arrive

### 4.3 Barcode Scanning
Use the Barcode Scanner page (`/barcode-scanner`) to:
- Scan parts to check inventory
- Log parts to a job by scanning
- Verify part compatibility

## Chapter 5: Service Guides & Documentation

### 5.1 Accessing Repair Guides (`/technician-portal/guides`)
The platform provides a library of:
- Step-by-step repair procedures
- Wiring diagrams
- Torque specifications
- OEM technical bulletins

### 5.2 AR Repair Guide (`/ar-repair-guide`)
For supported repairs, the AR guide overlays instructions directly on the vehicle:
1. Open the AR Repair Guide
2. Point your device at the vehicle component
3. AR overlays show step-by-step instructions

### 5.3 OEM Software (`/technician-portal/software`)
Access licensed OEM diagnostic software:
1. Go to **Software** in your portal
2. Select the vehicle manufacturer
3. Launch the appropriate diagnostic tool
4. Follow OEM procedures

## Chapter 6: Job Documentation

### 6.1 Uploading Photos
Good documentation protects you and the customer:
1. Open job card → **Documentation** tab
2. Click **Upload Photo**
3. Take photos of: damage found, parts replaced, before/after
4. Add caption/description to each photo

### 6.2 Digital Vehicle Walkaround
Before starting work:
1. Go to **Digital Vehicle Walkaround** (`/digital-vehicle-walkaround`)
2. Photograph all existing damage on the vehicle
3. Customer or advisor signs off on pre-existing damage
4. This protects you from liability for pre-existing issues
